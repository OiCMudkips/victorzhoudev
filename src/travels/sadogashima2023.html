Walking across Sado Island

Written July 2023

I travelled to Japan in June 2023 with some friends. We all arrived to, and departed from, Japan on different days and flights. As a result, I had a couple of days to myself. I decided to spend the majority of my time on Sado Island (佐渡島). This blog entry details my time spent on the island.

Day 1: Walking up to the Donden Highland Lodge

I started day 1 in a hostel in Osaka. My goal for that day was to make it to my lodgings, the Donden Highland Lodge on top of Sado Island, a fairly ambitious goal.

I left my hostel at around 6:30 AM to head to Osaka Itami Airport for my flight to Niigata. I thought I was in for a long train ride from Nishinari Ward, but thanks to the power of Google Maps, I was able to find and ride a convenient airport bus from Doubutsuen-mae Station. At the airport, I got some mackerel with rice for breakfast.

The flight to Niigata was pretty uneventful, and we landed at around 9:40 AM. At Niigata Airport, I had to get to the jetfoil ferry terminal to Sado Island. I thought that I would have to take a bus to Niigata Station, then transfer to a local bus to the ferry terminal, but through some last-minute research, I found out there was a shuttle straight to the ferry terminal. Here is the information for that shuttle for 2023:

  • English
    • As of writing, the English site has an incorrect adult fare listed. The fare is actually 700 yen.
  • Japanese

If you choose to take this shuttle, make sure to bring some exact change. I think the driver might be able to provide a small amount of change, but it didn't look like he had a lot of coins.

I was the only passenger headed to the ferry terminal that morning. The driver asked me if I was hiking (I was!) and talked about the time he had spent on Sado Island when he was younger. I didn't understand a bunch of it, but it was pretty cute! We got to the ferry terminal on time, and I got a gyuudon at the cafeteria there.
